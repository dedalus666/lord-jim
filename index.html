The Story

What happens when
one moment defines
your entire life?

For Jim, a young sailor with dreams of heroism, that moment comes when he abandons 800 Muslim pilgrims aboard what he believes is a sinking ship. The passengers survive. Jim's honor does not.

"He's one of us — a sailor born with honor in his bones."

Jim doesn't run from justice — he faces the maritime court with dignity, accepts his punishment, and surrenders his sailor's credentials. But standing trial can't erase the guilt and shame. Everywhere he turns, he's met with whispers: coward, deserter, the man who jumped.

The seasoned sailor Marlow sees Jim as "one of us" and recognizes his lost potential. Events take a turn when Marlow's friend, an elderly German butterfly collector, offers Jim refuge in Patusan — a remote jungle settlement where no one knows his past. There, among the Bugis people, Jim transforms into Lord Jim — beloved leader, warrior, tactical genius, and finally the hero he always dreamed of being.

But when ruthless pirate Gentleman Brown arrives, the guilt of Jim's past returns with devastating consequences. Jim's choice to show mercy triggers a massacre. The result is inevitable.

"In the destructive element immerse."
— Stein's Wisdom

Lord Jim is a sweeping rock opera combining the literary depth of Conrad with the visceral power of progressive rock. Through 22 original songs ranging from intimate ballads to full-company anthems, this is a story about the weight of a single choice, the cost of redemption, and what it means to finally stop running from who you are.

07Brierly's Ghost

Synopsis

Captain Brierly — a decorated judge at Jim's inquiry with a perfect twenty-year record — watches Jim's trial and is forced to confront an unbearable question: Would I have jumped too? Unable to shake the possibility, he is destroyed by self-doubt. Days after the trial, he steps into the ocean. This dark soliloquy reveals that the line between hero and coward may be far thinner than anyone wants to believe.
